## Executive Summary

Nicholas Eftimiades, drawing from a proprietary database of nearly 900 Chinese espionage cases, reveals a fundamentally different intelligence paradigm than Western agencies face. China employs a **whole-of-society approach**—mobilizing 150,000 state-owned enterprises, private companies, universities, diaspora communities, and ordinary citizens—all legally compelled under national security law to provide intelligence. The primary mission is always **protection of the Chinese Communist Party**, with outward-facing espionage (military, political, economic) as a secondary function.

## Case Studies

### Failed Tradecraft

- **Su Jianfeng** — Tried buying radiation-hardened chips claiming "car applications." Cover story impossible ($2500/chip vs $60 commercial). Fake company "Meditech" registered to his Liverpool restaurant. Arrested in DHS Bangkok sting.
- **Kevin Mallory** — Former CIA/DIA officer. Recruited via LinkedIn. Had FedEx clerk copy classified documents. MSS-issued phone destruction app malfunctioned—FBI found evidence.
- **Jerry Lee** — Former CIA case officer. Kept notebook with true names of all CIA assets in China. FBI surreptitious room entry discovered it. Contributed to 2010 mass compromise.

### Sophisticated Tradecraft

- **Dixon Yeo** — Recruited by professor at National University of Singapore. MSS bypassed customs (no passport stamp). Rotating WeChat accounts, cafe IPs. Set up fake company "Resolute." Collected 400+ TS/SCI clearance holder resumes. Never caught by tradecraft failure.
- **Christine Fang (方芳)** — Placed at San Francisco Bay University. Rose to run 2 student associations. Embedded in California politics. Tied to ~40 US politicians including a congressman who ran for president. Regular MSS case officer meetings at SF consulate. Discovered only when FBI gave defensive briefing.
- **Mike Sun / Chen Jie** — UFWD-funded ($80K) Arcadia city council candidate operation. Same home address as candidate. Attempted to weaponize IRS against Falun Gong. FBI sting.
- **Wang Shujun** — MSS asset inside Chinese democracy NGO in NYC since ~2006. 15+ years reporting on dissidents undetected.

### Extraordinary Renditions

- **Causeway Bookstore (2015)** — Published books on Xi Jinping's family. Owners forcibly rendered from Hong Kong and Thailand to mainland China.

## Cultural & Coercive Dynamics

- **Confucian subtlety**: "There might be trouble... it might be difficult otherwise" = explicit threat in Chinese cultural context
- **Patriotic education from age 6**: Children learn camouflage, weapons handling, grenade-diving
- **Blended motivation**: Assets act from genuine belief *and* coercion; difficult to distinguish
- **Open coordination**: MSS emails openly to hotel managers requesting cooperation—normalized intelligence-gathering culture

## Strategic Outlook

> "Even hunting cases one by one isn't stopping it, isn't even slowing it down." — Nicholas Eftimiades

The core problem identified is structural: Western agencies (CIA, FBI, NSA, etc.) follow a **whole-of-government** model—siloed, legalistic, bounded by civil liberties. China operates a **whole-of-society** model where the line between state, corporation, and citizen does not exist for intelligence purposes. The West wins individual cases but lacks the architecture to counter a system designed for infinite scale and generational patience.
